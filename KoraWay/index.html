As a first-generation Malian-Senegalese student, I was the first in my family to attend college — building my path while laying one down for those who would come after me. It was never just my struggle.

I watched my cousin make that same journey, longer and further — from Senegal to Canada. He had family waiting on the other side, people who loved him. And still, he struggled to find his footing. Those words stayed with me. I knew that feeling — but his was deeper. A new country, a new language, a new life, all at once.

KoraWay is named after the Kora — a West African instrument that carries stories across generations — and Way, for the paths we cultivate for ourselves and for those who come after. It exists to bridge the diaspora and back home. To make sure no one arrives alone. To help students build community and learn to navigate their city before diving headfirst into four years of college.

How It Works

Two sides.
One shared journey.

KoraWay connects African students with vetted Boston host families through a structured, AI-assisted experience — from the first match to the final week of the hosting period.

1

Student browses AI-matched host families

Each host card shows an AI compatibility score, a personalised reasoning blurb, strengths and considerations specific to the student's profile, the host's rating, student reviews, and a full bio.

AI-assisted
2

Student sends a hosting request

The host receives the student's bio and support needs. They can review the profile and choose to accept or decline. Once accepted, the experience begins.

Two-sided match
3

AI generates a shared arrival plan

On acceptance, AI creates a personalised 4-week plan based on the student's country, language, school, and needs. Both the host and student can leave comments on each task and track progress together.

AI-assisted
4

Student and host message each other directly

An integrated messaging thread keeps both sides connected throughout the hosting period — from pre-arrival questions to daily check-ins.

Real-time
5

Both sides have an AI assistant

Students can ask about banking, housing, campus life, and what to expect. Hosts can ask how to prepare for their specific student — cultural context, language, and practical advice.

AI-assisted
6

Reputation is tracked and enforced

Both parties rate the experience at the end of the hosting period. Hosts with ratings below 3.8 are paused and reviewed. Hosts can also track their hours contributed through completed plan tasks and logged sessions.

The Model

Where it is.
Where it's going.

KoraWay is currently a working demo — a fully functional prototype of both the student and host experience. The next step is putting it in front of real users, collecting data, and building toward a production-ready platform.

Stage 1 — Built

A fully functional demo covering the complete student and host experience — AI-powered host matching, shared arrival plan, real-time messaging, and AI assistants on both sides.

Stage 2 — Test & Learn

Launch to a small pilot cohort in Boston. Collect user feedback and behavioural data. Apply data science methods to identify friction points and unmet needs. Conduct expanded market research with African student communities.

Stage 3 — Full Product & Expand

Build toward a production-ready platform. Partner with Boston universities to integrate KoraWay as a pre-arrival resource for incoming international students. Expand nationally, then internationally.

Vetting Process

Supply is the product.
Vetting is the moat.

⚠ Vetting is not yet reflected in the demo — this outlines the trust and safety framework designed for the live product.

Every host passes a five-step verification before appearing in any student's match results.

Identity verification

Government ID cross-referenced with address and phone. No anonymous profiles on the supply side.

Home environment review

Video walkthrough. Minimum standards: private room, reliable internet, transit proximity.

Language and cultural screen

15-minute call assessing language proficiency and cultural familiarity with the student's country of origin.

Reference check

Two references. At least one from a student, academic, or community organisation they've supported.

Orientation and agreement

45-minute onboarding covering platform expectations, hosting structure, and escalation procedures.

Reputation System

Dual-direction ratings

Both parties rate at the end of the hosting period. Weighted by completion rate and task progress.

Activity completion score

Each host builds a verified completion score based on plan tasks and logged sessions. High scorers rank higher in AI matching.

Threshold enforcement

Hosts below 3.8 are automatically paused and reviewed within 5 business days.

Community endorsements

Endorsements from university offices carry 2× weight in matching scores.

Risk Analysis

Known risks and
how we're addressing them

Supply Risk
Host family acquisition
Two-sided marketplaces live and die on supply quality. Acquiring enough vetted hosts before launch is the hardest operational challenge — students can't be matched without them.
MitigationPartner with diaspora organisations, African student associations, and university international offices in Boston to seed the initial host network before any student-facing launch.
Adoption Risk
Reaching the right students
Building the product doesn't guarantee students will find it. Trust in a new platform with no existing user base has to be earned — especially in a community where word of mouth carries significant weight.
MitigationStart with direct outreach to African student communities at Northeastern and BU. Build trust through peer referrals and university endorsements rather than cold acquisition.
Scale Risk
Maintaining quality as volume grows
The product's value is personalisation and human connection, both of which are harder to maintain as volume grows. As volume increases, there is a risk of reduced quality and integrity of the platform.
MitigationCap student intake relative to available hosts early on. Use feedback data to monitor plan and match quality over time.
